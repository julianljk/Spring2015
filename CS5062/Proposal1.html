Iron Deficiency Chlorosis Identifier

Proposed by Julian Leong

Problem: Iron Deficiency Chlorosis is caused by the inability of plants to absorb iron from the soil, which causes a reduction in chlorophyll production that in turn reduces yield, and even plant death. Agronomists and farmers alike use a visual rating scale from 1-5 in order to identify the severity of iron deficiency chlorosis. This is a very tedious, time consuming, and error prone method. It would take hundreds of man-hours to assess a plot of crop.

Solution: To resolve this issue, a high-throughput image analysis software was developed. This software is able to capture the percentage of IDC infected area and the corresponding IDC rating through digital imaging. This framework is based on the notion of analyzing the color maps on the image, as well as utilizing the Naive Bayes Classification method in order to find a correlation between the percentages of IDC (from imaging) and the IDC score, allowing for an automated classification of IDC score. To enhance user experience and data acquisition, an Android application is proposed to be developed, as users will be able to take pictures with their phones and obtain the IDC rating instantly.

For a more detailed explaination, a link to a powerpoint presentation regarding the project is provided. Credits to Dr. Baskar Ganapathysubramaniam, Marcus Naik and the Department of Mechanical Engineering of Iowa State University

Essential features:

  • Mobile Android Application
  • Image pre-processing to remove noise
  • Image processing to identify a certain range of RGB values
  • Processing to identify potency of IDC

Additional features:

  • Move computational work to an external server or cloud to preserve battery life
  • Development of a database to store previously analyzed pictures along with the metadata associated to it
  • Robust UI
  • Precision in processing algorithms

Potential Issues: 

  • As camera sensors differ from phone to phone, there may be calibration issues across phones.

Platform and development: The client has created a basic working version of this softtware in Matlab, the problem lies in translating it over onto an android platform (which runs on Java). There are various libraries that exist for Java for image processing such as Marvin, ImageJ and Open CV. This project would also allow students to learn to use image processing tools in tandem with the Android platform.

Customer: Marcus Naik is a student at Iowa State University, currently pursuing his M.S. degree in Mechanical Engineering. He joined the Computational Mechanics and Physics lab research group in his junior year of his undergraduate degree, assisting the Agronomy Department at Iowa State by investigating the correlation between plant root traits and plant yield using similar image processing methods. He has also worked on polymer morphology in the past, investigating how coating different geometries with diblock copolymers affect the behavior of the polymer, utilizing the supercomputer at Iowa State (CyEnce) to perform simulations.

Required Skillset: As the client has specified the application to be on an Android platform, developers experienced in Android development would be essential. Aside from that, proficient knowledge in image processing is essential for the pre-processing and processing of the photos.

Work Justification:  Image processing is not a very common area of expertise, thus, it may take some time to get things right in regard to image processing. Aside from that, moving the processing from the phone itself to an external processor is a large undertaking on its own. Same goes with developing a database to store the necessary data.
